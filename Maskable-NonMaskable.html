MASKABLE &
NON-MASKABLE....

MASKABLE

*Maskable Interrupt Is A Hardware Interrupt That Can Be Disabled Or Ignored By The Instructions Of CPU.
*It Can Be Handled After Executing The Current Instruction.
*It Helps To Handle Lower Priority Tasks.
*Used To Interface With Peripheral Device.
*Response Time Is High.
*It May Be Vectored Or Non-Vectored.
*Operation Can Be Masked Or Made Pending.
*EX-RST6.5, RST7.5, And RST5.5 Of 8085.

NON-MASKABLE

*A Non-Maskable Interrupt Is A Hardware Interrupt That Cannot Be Disabled Or Ignored By The Instructions Of CPU.
*The Current Instructions And Status Are Stored In Stack For The CPU To Handle The Interrupt.
*Help To Handle Higher Priority Tasks
*Used For Emergency Purpose Ex- Power Failure,Smoke Detector.
*Response Time Is Low.
*All Are Vectored Interrupts.
*Operation Cannot Be Masked Or Made Pending.
*Ex-Trap Of 8085 Microprocessor.
